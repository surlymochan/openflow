# Super-Assistant Comparison

Use this page when someone asks whether openflow should absorb Super-Assistant's
enterprise delivery harness or split separate personal/team workflows.

## Verdict

Super-Assistant has useful enterprise ingredients: team constraints, checkpoint
notifications, plugin execution, an experience index, and S3-backed team sync.
Those ideas are worth absorbing.

openflow should not absorb the whole harness. After the policy-overlay
integration, xflow is stronger because the same capabilities are expressed as
thin atoms, provider seams, and structured proof artifacts on top of the
existing `yolo` and `corps` tracks.

```text
Super-Assistant = enterprise harness and connector inventory
xflow = workflow runtime plus policy overlay and atomized proof
```

## Scorecard

`76/100`. Super-Assistant still has broader concrete enterprise connector
coverage, but xflow now has the cleaner runtime contract.

## What xflow Absorbed

- `team_constraints.py` becomes `.xflow/profile.json`,
  `.xflow/team-policy.json`, and `policy_effective.json`. Numeric thresholds
  take the stricter value, lists merge and dedupe, and team strings/booleans
  override personal defaults.
- Demand profiling becomes `workflow_recommendation.json`, generated by
  `xflow intake recommend`, and remains advisory input rather than a workflow
  bypass.
- Checkpoint notification becomes `notify.checkpoint`, which writes
  `notification/checkpoint-*.json` and explicitly records
  `approves_gate=false`.
- Plugin, storage, and experience reuse become `plugin.run`, `storage.sync`,
  and `experience.digest`. The first provider set is `file`/`mock`/dry-run
  safe, so DingTalk/S3-style secrets stay outside the core.
- Team enforcement becomes `policy.gate`, which emits structured
  `failed_checks` for coverage, reviewer, smoke, and review-artifact
  requirements.

## Why Not Fork Team Workflows

Do not create `team-yolo` or `personal-yolo`. That would duplicate workflow
YAML and drift as soon as a phase changes.

The better split is execution mode:

- `personal`: default, local-first, no external providers required.
- `team`: loads the team policy, enables optional notification/experience
  atoms, and checks stricter gates.

The workflow authority stays with `workflows/yolo.yaml`,
`workflows/corps.yaml`, the atom registry, and proof artifacts. External
systems may inform the run, but they do not advance state by themselves.

## Boundary

xflow should keep Super-Assistant's domain lessons, not its coupling model.
Concrete enterprise providers can be added later, but the first-class contract
must remain: notification is not approval, plugins are not workflow state, and
experience is advisory context rather than a gate substitute.
